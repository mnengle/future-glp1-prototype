**Compiled: April 2026**
Source: Direct pricing pages and third-party review aggregators.

## Key Competitive Observations

1. **Hims is the cheapest for semaglutide ongoing** at $175/mo flat, but requires multi-month prepay for lowest rates.
2. **Mochi bundles membership+med** at a competitive total ($178 sema, $278 tirz) but requires ongoing subscription.
3. **Our pricing mirrors Medvi** exactly. This is intentional given we're prototyping the Medvi model, but it's not competitive against Hims or Mochi on pure cost.
4. **Ro Body Program was replaced by Noom as of April 2026.** Pricing shown reflects the final Ro Body rates.
5. **Henry Meds pricing scales with dose** — the advertised $99 rate is starting dose only; most patients end up at $249–$397 monthly at therapeutic doses.

## Future's Differentiation

On pure medication cost, Future is mid-pack. The differentiator is the **bundled coaching referral (first month free with Future)** and the **resistance training program** to preserve muscle during GLP-1 therapy. No other provider in this market offers this.

## Regulatory Context (April 2026)

- FDA issued warning letters to Henry Meds and others in March 2026 about marketing compounded products as equivalent to FDA-approved drugs.
- Hims withdrew its $49 compounded Wegovy pill under FDA/HHS pressure.
- Novo Nordisk is litigating against Hims over compounded semaglutide.
- Compounded GLP-1s are now under narrower 503A/503B compounding permissions following the FDA's shortage resolution.

## Recommended Adjustments for Future's Pricing Strategy

1. **Keep current medication pricing** ($179/$279 first mo, $299/$399 ongoing) but lead with coaching value, not price.
2. **Consider adding a "Medication-only" tier** at Hims-competitive rates if we want to compete on price.
3. **The "first month free with Future coaching" hook** is the real competitive moat. Lead the marketing with this.
